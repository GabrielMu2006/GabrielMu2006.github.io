Final Review Skill is a Codex skill for turning scattered course materials into a structured final-exam review package. It is designed for situations where slides, PDFs, assignments, past exams, and notes need to become one practical study system.

**Insight.** The strongest idea in the README is that exam preparation is not just summarization. It is a pipeline from messy source materials to an actionable review workflow: organize the knowledge, surface high-frequency exam points, preserve formulas and methods, add self-testing, and keep a processing log so the student can trust what was covered.

**Highlights.**

- Builds structured outlines from course materials, including concepts, formulas, methods, conditions, common mistakes, and exam patterns.
- Uses MarkItDown when available, while checking conversion quality and falling back to original files when conversion is unreliable.
- Produces review outlines, chapter question banks, mock exams, answers, grading points, and material-processing logs.
- Emphasizes self-testing by adding judgment questions, classic examples, short-answer tasks, calculations, and multiple mock papers.
